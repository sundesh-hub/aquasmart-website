Why this exists

Karachi's water math doesn't close

The city's municipal network supplies roughly 550–650 million gallons a day, against a demand of over 1,200 MGD. The shortfall gets filled by unregulated tanker networks — expensive, and with no guarantee of what's actually in the water.

Rainwater harvesting alone isn't enough either: Karachi's entire monsoon lands in a four-to-six-week window, leaving RWH systems idle the rest of the year. Meanwhile the same coastal climate holds 60–80% humidity nearly year-round — a second water source most systems never touch.

Municipal supply550–650 MGD
Urban demand1,200+ MGD
The gap is filled informally — at a cost, and without quality assurance. AquaSmart targets the household scale of that gap directly.
How it works

Every drop is judged before it's stored

Rain and humid air are both routed through the same quality checkpoint. Nothing reaches the storage tank until pH, turbidity, and TDS sensors agree it's safe.

Under the hood

Built on a two-controller, cloud-connected stack

An ESP32 master handles connectivity and decision logic; an Arduino UNO runs the sensor hub. Everything syncs to the cloud in near real time.

Master controller
ESP32, dual-core 240MHz, Wi-Fi
Sensor hub
Arduino UNO (ATmega328P)
AWG element
TEC1-17310 Peltier ×2
Water routing
12V solenoid valves + opto-relays
Cloud database
MongoDB, time-series telemetry
API layer
Node.js / Express REST + MQTT
Mobile app
React Native, iOS & Android
Admin portal
React.js + Node.js, real-time device control

Mobile app

Your tank, your readings, your control

A single cross-platform app for the household, and a separate admin web portal for fleet-level device management.

  • → Live pH, turbidity, and TDS readings, updated in near real time
  • → Push alerts the moment contamination or a system fault is detected
  • → Manual valve override and AWG mode control
  • → Filter history and maintenance reminders
  • → Admin web portal for multi-device, multi-user oversight
